About SpectroCough

SpectroCough is a state-of-the-art Diagnostic Support, Pre-screening System engineered to bridge the gap between acoustic signal processing and deep learning. It functions as a non-invasive, rapid pre-screening tool that analyzes respiratory audio signatures to identify distinct pathological patterns.

The Resolution

Contactless Screening

Eliminates the risk of infectious transmission between patient and doctor during initial diagnosis.

Latency Reduction

Reduces diagnosis time from hours (traditional lab tests) to seconds using Edge AI inference.

Non-Invasive Analysis

Analyzes cough acoustics without blood tests, imaging, or any physical contact.

AI Assisted Decisions

Combines deep learning with acoustic biomarkers to support rapid respiratory pre-screening.

Explainable Predictions

Provides confidence scores, acoustic deviations, visual evidence and AI-generated explanations.

Early Risk Identification

Prioritizes potentially affected individuals for further clinical investigation and medical attention.


The Architecture

1. Audio Acquisition

Respiratory cough audio is captured through either a digital stethoscope or a standard microphone.

2. Pre-processing

Audio is standardized, normalized, denoised and trimmed to remove irrelevant silence.

3. Feature Extraction

MFCC, RMS, ZCR, Spectral Centroid, Bandwidth and Roll-off are extracted.

4. Spectrogram Generation

Mel-Spectrogram representations are generated to capture time-frequency information.

5. Hybrid AI Inference

Deep CNN and Dense Neural Networks classify respiratory conditions from extracted features.

6. Confidence Analysis

The system computes disease probabilities and identifies the highest confidence prediction.

7. Explainable AI

Dynamic LLM explanations describe acoustic biomarkers in both scientific and layman terminology.

8. Counterfactual Logic

The decision boundary engine identifies how acoustic features must change to reach another diagnosis.

9. Report Generation

Interactive reports combine probabilities, explanations, acoustic metrics and AI interpretations.
